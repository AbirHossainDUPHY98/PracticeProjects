Validating the Hypothesis: R&D Budget Converts Into National Growth
Economics & Policy Statistical Modeling R&D Patents

A seemingly natural intuition drives the idea behind this study, which tries to find the patterns in multi-dimensional data to seek validation to that idea. Here statistical correlations are measured between - RnD spending and patent output as a measure of productive technical growth - ultimately converting into economic growth for nations.

Statistical Correlation Between Structural Properties & Emergent Nature of Proteins
Biochemistry Computational Methods Statistical Modeling Protein

This project models functional properties of proteins to their chemical and structural properties. Here 30,000 proteins from 3 different mammalian organisms are compared following the idea of finding some patterns, which might be unforeseen by the naked eye.

A Model for Projectile Motion Enhanced with Varying Atmospheric Density Data
Physics & Simulation Computational Methods

From programming a standard solution to the ordinary projectile motion equations(2D), this project tries to get the simulation conditions closer to reality. By merging atmospheric density data with the barometric approximation of density, an atmospheric density model was created. That was later used to simulate the projectile motion under more realistic conditions.
